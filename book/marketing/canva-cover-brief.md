# Book Cover Design Brief

**Book Title:** AI for the Rest of Us
**Subtitle:** 12 Things You Need to Know Before Everyone Else Figures It Out
**Author:** [Your Name]

---

## Dimensions

- **Print-ready size:** 1600 x 2560 pixels (KDP recommended for e-book covers)
- **Safe zone:** Keep all critical text and elements at least 50px from any edge

## Color Palettes

Avoid the typical dark blue / black / neon green "techy" look. The cover should feel friendly, modern, and approachable — like a smart friend explaining something, not a computer science textbook.

### Palette A — Warm Coral + Cream
- Primary: Coral / Soft Red (#E8654A)
- Secondary: Warm Cream (#FFF5E1)
- Accent: Charcoal for text (#2D2D2D)
- Feeling: Warm, inviting, stands out in thumbnail grids

### Palette B — Teal + Soft Yellow
- Primary: Muted Teal (#2A9D8F)
- Secondary: Pale Yellow (#FAFAD2)
- Accent: Dark Slate (#264653)
- Feeling: Fresh, calming, approachable but still smart

### Palette C — Lavender + White
- Primary: Soft Lavender (#9B8EC5)
- Secondary: Clean White (#FFFFFF)
- Accent: Dark Purple-Gray (#3C3354)
- Feeling: Modern, gentle, unexpected for a tech book (which is the point)

## Typography

Use Google Fonts for broad compatibility and easy Canva access.

| Element | Suggested Fonts | Style |
|---------|----------------|-------|
| **Title** | **Poppins Bold** or **Nunito Black** | Large, heavy weight. Must be readable at thumbnail size (think: phone screen in an Amazon search result). |
| **Subtitle** | **Source Sans Pro** or **Work Sans Medium** | Clean, lighter weight than title. Should not compete with the title. |
| **Author Name** | Same as subtitle font, regular weight | Small, at the bottom. Not the star of the show. |

## Layout

```
┌──────────────────────────┐
│                          │
│   AI FOR THE             │
│   REST OF US             │  <-- Title: LARGE, top third
│                          │
│   12 Things You Need     │
│   to Know Before         │  <-- Subtitle: medium, middle area
│   Everyone Else          │
│   Figures It Out         │
│                          │
│      [ simple icon ]     │  <-- Visual element: centered, not busy
│                          │
│                          │
│      [Your Name]         │  <-- Author name: small, bottom
│                          │
└──────────────────────────┘
```

- **Title** occupies the top third. Big, bold, unmissable.
- **Subtitle** sits below the title with comfortable spacing.
- **Visual element** is minimal and centered. One icon or shape, not a collage.
- **Author name** is small and near the bottom.
- Plenty of whitespace. The cover should breathe.

## Visual Concept

The goal is "approachable and for normal people." Think of the visual as a small, friendly accent — not the main event.

**Do:**
- A simple, friendly robot icon (round, smiling, minimal)
- A lightbulb with a clean, modern line-art style
- Abstract geometric shapes (circles, rounded rectangles) suggesting connection
- A single chat bubble or speech bubble icon
- A simple "power on" symbol reimagined in a playful way

**Do NOT:**
- Terminator / menacing robot imagery
- A brain with circuits or wires
- Matrix-style falling code
- Dark, moody, sci-fi aesthetics
- Stock photos of any kind
- Busy illustrations with too many elements

## Comp Titles (Cover References)

Look at these successful covers in the beginner-friendly / tech-for-everyone space for tone and feel:

1. **"Steal Like an Artist" by Austin Kleon** — Bold title, simple icon, lots of whitespace
2. **"Make Time" by Jake Knapp & John Zeratsky** — Bright color, clean typography, minimal illustration
3. **"Show Your Work!" by Austin Kleon** — High contrast, hand-drawn feel, approachable
4. **"Everybody Writes" by Ann Handley** — Clean, modern, professional but not corporate
5. **"The Non-Designer's Design Book" by Robin Williams** — Friendly, educational, clear hierarchy

The common thread: bold title, one or two colors, simple visual, confidence without intimidation.

## Canva Tips

1. **Start with a template:** In Canva, search for "ebook cover" or "kindle cover" and filter by the 1600x2560 dimension. Pick a clean, minimal template as your starting point.
2. **Resize check:** After designing, zoom out to 25% to simulate how the cover looks as a thumbnail. If you cannot read the title, make it bigger.
3. **Use Canva's built-in icons:** Search for "robot," "lightbulb," or "chat bubble" in the Elements panel. Filter by "line art" or "flat" style for the cleanest look.
4. **Font pairing:** Canva has a "Font combinations" feature under Text — use it to sanity-check your choices.
5. **Export as PNG** at highest quality for upload to KDP and Gumroad.
6. **Create a mockup version** too: search "book mockup" in Canva to place your cover on a 3D book image for social media promotion.
